CATCH UP

THIS SUMMER

It was a tough school year for everyone. You and your student need some downtime this summer, but it’s also a valuable time to get back on track. Fusion Academy offers a convenient and safe option to remediate classes or rebuild a solid learning foundation. Fit your learning around vacations, sports, or whatever fun you have planned; Fusion makes both learning and leisure possible.

SKILL LAUNCH

SUMMER CAMPS

$500 | 1 hour per day for one week (Mon-Fri)

Join us for our skill launch camps focused on five key skills for successful students. Each camp is taught in our unique one-to-one model: one student and one teacher per classroom.

Camp topics:

Finance: Become a money-wise young adult ready to enter the world. Topics include: budgets, banking, loans, savings, and taxes.

Study Skills: Learn the Top 5 Study Hacks you can use for all your classes to study smarter, not harder.

Modern Etiquette: Learn and practice the skills to succeed in virtual, social, work, and school life.

Organization & More: Get a crash course in refining your executive functioning skills to improve all parts of your life from home to school to friendships.

Crush Your College Essay: Work with our expert Post-Secondary Counselors on one of the most important essays you will write - your college admissions essay.

Crushing Your College Admissions for Seniors: Are you a rising senior getting ready to apply for college? Work one-to-one with a Fusion Post-Secondary counselor to learn about the college admissions process and get guidance on where to begin.

Jumpstart Juniors: Junior year in high school is a pivotal time to prep for college admissions. Work one-to-one with a Fusion Post-Secondary Counselor on topics like creating a college timeline, assessing college preferences, reviewing the differences between the SAT and ACT, and major/college exploration.

Freshman & Sophomore College Admission Start-up: It’s never too early to start thinking about college admissions! Ninth and tenth grade students work one-to-one with a Fusion Post-Secondary Counselor on reviewing the college timeline, discussing the differences between the SAT and ACT, creating an academic plan and ways to bolster your high school resume.
